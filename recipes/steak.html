Classic Steak with Garlic Butter

Main Page

Ingredients:

For the Steak:

  • 2 ribeye steaks (about 1 to 1 ½ inches thick)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary or thyme

For the Garlic Butter Sauce:

  • 4 tablespoons butter, softened
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, finely chopped
  • 1 teaspoon lemon juice (optional)
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste

Instructions:

1. Prepare the Steaks:

  1. Remove the steaks from the refrigerator about 30-60 minutes before cooking to bring them to room temperature. Pat them dry with paper towels.
  2. Generously season both sides of the steaks with salt and freshly ground black pepper.

2. Make the Garlic Butter Sauce:

  1. In a small bowl, combine the softened butter, minced garlic, fresh parsley, lemon juice (if using), salt, and pepper. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly incorporated. Set aside.

3. Cook the Steaks:

  1. Heat a large cast-iron skillet or heavy-bottomed pan over high heat until it is very hot.
  2. Add the olive oil to the skillet. When the oil is shimmering and hot, add the steaks. Sear the steaks for 2-3 minutes on each side, or until a nice crust forms.
  3. Reduce the heat to medium and add the 2 tablespoons of butter, smashed garlic cloves, and rosemary or thyme sprigs to the skillet.
  4. Tilt the skillet slightly and use a spoon to baste the steaks with the melted butter and garlic-herb mixture. Continue to cook for another 2-3 minutes on each side for medium-rare, or until the steaks reach your desired level of doneness. Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature: 130°F (54°C) for medium-rare, 140°F (60°C) for medium.

4. Rest the Steaks:

  1. Transfer the steaks to a plate and let them rest for 5-10 minutes. This allows the juices to redistribute and results in a more flavorful and tender steak.

5. Serve:

  1. Top each steak with a generous dollop of the garlic butter sauce.
  2. Serve immediately, accompanied by your favorite sides such as mashed potatoes, steamed vegetables, or a fresh salad.

Enjoy your steak!
