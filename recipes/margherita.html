Classic Margherita Pizza

Main Page

Ingredients:

For The Dough:

  • 2 ¼ teaspoons active dry yeast (1 packet)
  • 1 ½ cups warm water (110°F or 45°C)
  • 3 ½ to 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar

For The Sauce:

  • 1 can (14 ounces) whole peeled tomatoes
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional)

For The Topping:

  • 8 ounces fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced
  • 1 bunch fresh basil leaves
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

1. Make the Dough:

  1. In a small bowl, dissolve the yeast and sugar in warm water. Let it sit for about 5-10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
  2. In a large bowl, combine 3 ½ cups of flour and salt. Make a well in the center and add the yeast mixture and olive oil.
  3. Mix until the dough starts to come together. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 8-10 minutes, adding more flour as needed, until the dough is smooth and elastic.
  4. Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1-2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.

2. Prepare the Sauce:

  1. In a saucepan, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté until fragrant but not browned, about 1 minute.
  2. Add the canned tomatoes, breaking them up with a spoon. Let the sauce simmer for about 15-20 minutes until it thickens slightly.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and oregano (if using). You can add a teaspoon of sugar if the sauce is too acidic. Let the sauce cool.

3. Assemble the Pizza:

  1. Preheat your oven to its highest setting, usually around 500°F (260°C). If you have a pizza stone, place it in the oven to heat up.
  2. Punch down the risen dough and divide it into two portions (or keep it whole for one large pizza). Roll out the dough on a floured surface to your desired thickness.
  3. Place the rolled-out dough on a piece of parchment paper if you're using a pizza stone, or on a baking sheet.
  4. Spread a thin layer of tomato sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges.
  5. Arrange the mozzarella slices evenly over the sauce.
  6. Bake the pizza in the preheated oven for about 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.

4. Finishing Touches

  1. Remove the pizza from the oven and immediately scatter fresh basil leaves over the top.
  2. Drizzle with a little olive oil and sprinkle with a pinch of salt.
  3. Slice and serve hot.

Enjoy your delicious homemade Margherita pizza!
